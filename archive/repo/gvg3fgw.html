There's someone who has been running a 30 day bacon only group, over on FB. It's been going for years. a report from 2016 -- https://www.dietdoctor.com/what-happens-when-you-eat-nothing-but-bacon-for-30-days-straight

The caveat we give here is that it's important to have fresh (as in unprocessed, uncured) meat for the vitamin C. from our FAQ, https://www.reddit.com/r/zerocarb/wiki/faq#wiki_what_about_vitamin_c.3F

When meat is brined, it destroys the vitamin C -- that's why sailors had scurvy.

Bacon isn't brined meat -- it's cured meat. But we don't know what happens to the vitamin C in pork belly which has a conventional cure (sodium nitrate, sodium erythrobate). So it makes sense to assume that there is 0 vitamin C in bacon and make sure to include fresh meat.

[Interestingly, in "naturally cured bacon" -- where the pork belly is cured with a a celery/cherry juice powder process --- there is vitamin C from the cherry powder.]

eta: Kelly Williams-Hogan will frost her burgers with bacon dripping when she wants fattier meat. Worth a try, see if it suits your appetite.
