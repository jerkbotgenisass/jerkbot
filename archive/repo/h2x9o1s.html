This is great for an advanced lifter like Jeff and I imagine it would work fine as long as you can recover from it and progress the weight you use for each movement over time.

to rant about the volume - volume (and everything else) imo is secondary to progressive tension overload. If you do 3 sets a week of bench, squat, deadlift, row, chins, ohp, etc (main compound lifts) and every week you add weight to the bar - that's better than 20 sets of each muscle or a program like this where you're not adding weight to the bar. In reality, it's hard to have one without the other, but it's important to realize this so you're not focusing on the wrong thing. IMO as a beginner, you should do less volume rather than more - focus on adding weight to the bar over time with standard good form. As you progress through your training career and become intermediate - one of the signs of which is that you will stall your lifts more frequently - this is the time to add volume, secondary exercises, advanced techniques like supersets/dropsets/rest pause/etc. Once your numbers are elite, you'll know what does and doesn't work for you by then and most likely you're not putting on much muscle at that point anyway if you're natural
