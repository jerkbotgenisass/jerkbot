Might you be overtraining?

"Overtraining syndrome (OTS) occurs where an athlete is training vigorously, yet performance deteriorates. One sign of OTS is suppressed immune function, with an increased incidence of upper respiratory tract infection (URTI). An increased incidence of URTIs is also associated with high volume/intensity training."

https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12696983/#:~:text=Overtraining%20syndrome%20(OTS)%20occurs%20where,respiratory%20tract%20infection%20(URTI).

Can you make space to have one or two days off where you just walk in the park? Or are you training for an upcoming competition?

Also, maybe eat a few eggs everyday as a "supplement". They've got some nutrients you don't typically get in meat (unless you're eating liver regularly) like choline and fat-soluble vitamins. Multivitamins won't help IMO because they're nowhere near as bioavailable as real food.
