1) You don't need cutting and bulking phases. That's broscience and a waste of time. The extra muscle you build while bulking will be lost when you go into your more aggressive cutting phase. For a deeper dive into this topic I recommend Greg Doucettes youtube videos. He has a bunch on this topic.

2) In the beginning everything works. It's called newbygains.

3) For me personally your routine contains way too much cardio but if you like that it's fine. Just make sure to not get into too big of a deficit. As a beginner I'd gravitate more towards a full body 3x a week schedule. Beginners usually benefit from higher frequency of given exercises and can rarely handle the high volume that comes with split routines.

Even if you decide to stay on a split it seems somewhat awkward to me. A Push / Pull / Leg split might be exactly what you are looking for. No need for extra "ab-workouts". Just do a core circuit at the end of every workout. The most effective exercises are hanging legraises (with your back against the wall), obliqueraises, reverse hyperextentions and hollowholds.

And please do yourself the favour of informing yourself about the cutting and bulking myth. It will safe you lots of time and frustration.
