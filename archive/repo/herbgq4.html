I actually think most of the top athletes aren’t on PEDs
Posted by teenwolf13. Posted at 18:26 UTC on September 29, 2021. 12 Points. (self.crossfit) (Permalink)

Here’s why:

I’m 38 and have been doing CrossFit for almost 10 years. I wasn’t an athlete growing up, I don’t have particularly impressive genetics, and I started “late”. In spite of that, I’m kind of fit. Top 20% in the open each year.

I only train 4-5 days a week, I eat well and sleep as well as someone with a two-year old can. Yesterday I only trained for 7 minutes and 19 seconds because it’s what I had time for. I’ve never taken any PEDs, don’t supplement, and haven’t even had caffeine in about 15 years.

With all of that, I’m still fitter 10 years later and improving incrementally year over year. It doesn’t feel impossible to me that people who dedicate their lives to exercise and nutrition can perform at an elite level without PEDs if I can be “above average” with all of the above constraints on my time and energy.

Obviously none of “know” anything beyond our own experience but I’m inclined to give the benefit of the doubt based on my experiences with CrossFit as a casual, but passionate, athlete.

TL;DR - I’m kind of fit and only try kind of hard. Seems to me that people who try really hard can be really fit without cheating.
