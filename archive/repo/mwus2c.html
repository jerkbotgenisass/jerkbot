Unpopular Opinion: 10% a week rule is setting people up for failure - the math behind the 10% a week rule.
Posted by JeffsBurnerAccount. Posted at 13:3 UTC on April 23, 2021. 0 Points. (self.running) (Permalink)

10 km a week compounded over a year is 1,291 km... Is this really a valid rule of thumb or is this setting beginners up for failure?

For me I feel like 10% a month up to a certain individual amount would be a better rule of thumb.

Say between 20 - 40 km a week. Noting that the level of health benefits between the two isn't substantial because the amount of activity for 20 km a week roughly meets CDC health guidelines of 75 minutes of cardio activity a week (assuming that running has a 2x factor which would put 75 minutes at 150 minutes).

Also, it seems monthly increases make more sense because it can take a month for bones and tendons to recover from the prior months of training.

It would probably be advisable to add in recovery weeks where you reduce your kms and then build them back up from there just to be even safer.

I feel like longevity outweighs progress when it comes to running because small injuries can set one back an easy month.
